#### What do I mean by the Wisdom of the Elders?

I volunteered for several years for Age Concern as an IT helper and I found it tragic in the following ways...

The tragedy of man is that as soon as he is built he is knocked down. In other words, by the time you are wise enough to see through this game you are in old age and about to die. This is like building a house and as soon as it is built knocking it down and starting to build a new one and doing that over and over again for generations and maybe, perversely that is what is happening in our world. So, I know "you can't put an old head on young shoulders", but, I think there is a ton of waste the way that life builds a man up to the point of being wise and then he dies and all that wisdom is lost and life has to start from blank ignorance again (a new born in other words). Arresting  this wastage involves consulting the elders and the lessons they learnt over the course of a lifetime. And that is what this is about. If you are willing to write your life story and put it in a book, then, I will link to it from here and it can be passed on to the youth. For a lot of older people, what the young ones are going through in life is transparent in terms of how it will work out for them because the older people had an equivalent experience when they were young. So, if you are elderly why not write up what your life has taught you and do something about not letting it go to waste. Wiser cultures of the past used to mandate that young people listened to the elders of the community but the brash society we live in now has largely dispensed with that and I think it needs to be brought back because the world is a paradox right now in that it's very clever but very naive at the same time.  

Another thing I want to say is that like many men find a woman more attractive with no makeup on because it's more honest I will find anything you write about your life experience much more genuine if you don't have it edited by an editor in the interest of making yourself look more polished and clean than you really are, or, it could be said, "in putting forward a false image of oneself". In other words, as the Nirvana song recommends "Come as you are" or as I say, "Don't try to make yourself look different to how you actually are using deceptions". To put it simply I think it's absolutely a valid project to have younger ones help older people communicate their life learnings either helping them with technology or simply recording what they have to say. I think it is basically arrestable the way that society treats with scant regard, those who have a lot to offer through a life time of experience. Someone elderly said to me, "Nothing much surprises me any more, I've seen it all" meaning that it's much easier to be surprised by life when you are young and naive than it is when you are older and experienced and are no longer naive about how when you are young you are being set up for a life of manipulation and exploitation. 

You might consider me wise. I might consider myself wise. If I am wise, how did I get there? I spent a lot of time from the age of 30 onwards (although before that as well) around older people and more importantly still, perhaps, I paid attention and I listened. I would admonish, therefore, that if you have ambitions to become wise (to this game) that you pay close attention to older people also. School can indoctrinate you but only life can educate you. Therefore if its an education you want who better to listen to than older people?
